Sustainable tips



Mountain

Click here to read.

Mountains have incredibly fragile ecosystems. They host unique wildlife, rare plant species, and delicate soils that need protection.

What can you do?
• Stay on marked trails: prevent soil erosion and protect plant habitats.
• Carry reusable gear: reduce plastic waste in remote areas.
• Respect wildlife: observe animals quietly and keep distance.
• Leave no trace: pack out trash, avoid picking plants, avoid unauthorized fires.

Why it matters?
Mountains act as water sources for cities and host unique biodiversity. Responsible travel prevents erosion and preserves habitats for future hikers.

Sea

Click here to read.

Coastal and marine environments are sensitive to pollution, over-tourism, and climate change, and coral reefs and marine ecosystems can be easily damaged

What can you do?
• Avoid single-use plastics: bring reusable bottles, cutlery, and shopping bags to reduce ocean pollution.
• Respect marine life: observe animals and coral without touching them.
• Choose eco-friendly operators: look for tours with environmental certifications.
• Conserve water & energy: reuse towels and shorten showers.

Why it matters?
Coasts provide resources and habitats for both wildlife and local communities dependent on the sea. Responsible travelers help protect fragile ecosystems, ensuring beaches and marine life thrive.

City

Click here to read.

City tourism presents challenges such as high population density, vehicle emissions, and intense infrastructure use.

What can you do?
• Use public transport: buses, trams, subways, and bike-sharing reduce emissions compared to taxis or cars.
• Support the local economy: eat at small restaurants, buy from local artisans.
• Be mindful of waste: recycle when possible and avoid single-use plastics.

Why it matters?
Responsible city tourism preserves cultural heritage, reduces pollution, and ensures tourism benefits local communities rather than large corporations. Travelers also gain more authentic experiences through respectful engagement.

Accommodation

Click here to read.

Accommodations have a great impact on your travel footprint: eco-friendly lodging not only minimizes environmental harm, but also supports the local community and provides a richer travel experience.

What can you do?
• Check certifications: look for certified accommodations.
• Prefer small and local lodgings: eco-lodges or family-run hotels help the local community and reduce environmental impact.
• Reduce energy use: reuse towels; turn off lights and devices when leaving the room.
• Minimize plastic use: bring reusable bags and avoid hotels heavily relying on single-use plastics.

Why It Matters?
Choosing sustainable accommodation encourages the hospitality industry to adopt environmentally friendly practices. It reduces water and energy consumption, lowers pollution, and supports local communities.
